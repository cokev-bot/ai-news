Summary: The artificial intelligence landscape is currently defined by an aggressive expansion of corporate influence, shifting geopolitical tensions, and massive capital inflows. In the United States, industry giants OpenAI and Anthropic are intensifying their political efforts by opening offices in Washington and increasing spending on lobbyists to influence federal lawmakers (NYT AI). This political pivot is mirrored by venture capital; Andreessen Horowitz has emerged as the largest known spender of the current campaign cycle (NYT AI). Financial markets continue to react strongly to the AI boom, though results vary by region. SoftBank reported a net income of $11.6 billion in the fourth quarter, bolstered by a $25 billion gain from its stake in OpenAI (FT AI). While European AI stocks are seeing a surge as global investors seek winners (FT AI), China's tech conglomerates, including Alibaba and Tencent, have largely been bypassed by investors favoring "pure AI plays" (FT AI). Meanwhile, the defense sector is seeing explosive growth, with AI-weapons startup Anduril reaching a $61 billion valuation after raising $5 billion in funding (NYT AI). Technological ambitions are also shifting toward automation and competition. A new venture called Recursive Superintelligence, backed by $4 billion and staffed by former Meta, Google, and OpenAI researchers, is working to develop self-improving AI (NYT AI). Simultaneously, Google DeepMind is positioning itself to challenge the dominance of Anthropic and OpenAI (FT AI). These developments are coinciding with high-stakes international diplomacy. Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang recently joined a U.S. delegation to Beijing accompanying President Trump (NYT AI). This visit highlights a critical tension, as both the U.S. and China are expected to discuss the risks associated with AI, yet neither nation appears willing to decelerate its progress (NYT AI). Amidst this growth, internal conflicts persist, such as the legal disputes between Elon Musk and Sam Altman regarding the original non-profit vision of OpenAI (FT AI).

AI Labs

Summary: The AI industry is shifting its focus toward "Agents at Scale," with a heavy emphasis on long-horizon autonomy and programmatic control for coding agents (BaiduAI_News). This trend is evidenced by the emergence of new performance metrics, such as Daily Active Agents (DAA), which Baidu proposed as a more accurate measure of ecosystem health and output than simple token consumption (BaiduAI_News). Significant updates to coding tools are enabling agents to operate with greater independence. Claude Code has introduced the `/goal` command and the "Ralph loop," which allows the agent to continue working until a specific completion condition is met (ClaudeDevs, ClaudeDevs, ClaudeDevs). To support these long-running tasks, Claude Code now offers an "auto mode" to eliminate human wait times (ClaudeDevs), a `/schedule` command for cadence-based runs (ClaudeDevs), and a `/loop` command for iterative refactors (ClaudeDevs). Additionally, "stop hooks" now provide programmatic control over when an agent is permitted to finish (ClaudeDevs). Other labs are similarly expanding agentic capabilities and accessibility. OpenAI's Codex now utilizes an in-app browser to test applications across various viewport sizes, utilizing screenshots and animation-disabling features to accelerate testing (OpenAIDevs). In the open-weights and model access space, Qwen3.6-Plus is currently available for free on the Nous Portal (Alibaba_Qwen), and Tensorix AI has launched four GLM models, including the agent-ready GLM-5-Turbo and the long-horizon focused GLM-5.1 (Zai_org). The industry is also refining how these complex systems are measured. Kimi Moonshot integrated FrontierCS into Harbor to release a long-horizon agent leaderboard, evaluating agents on tasks involving up to 835 turns and 200K output tokens (Kimi_Moonshot). Early results from this leaderboard show Kimi K2.6 leading with a score of 46.9, followed by Claude Code Opus 4.7 at 43.0 (Kimi_Moonshot). This push for higher autonomy is further supported by the availability of "Fast mode" for Opus 4.7 in research previews across several platforms, including Cursor and v0 (ClaudeDevs).

Developers

Summary: Major AI coding assistants are introducing advanced automation and testing capabilities to reduce manual oversight and accelerate the development lifecycle. Claude Code and OpenAI's Codex have both rolled out updates designed to handle long-running tasks and complex validation processes more autonomously. Claude Code has introduced several tools to ensure tasks are completed without constant user intervention. The new `/goal` command allows developers to set specific completion conditions—such as passing a lint step or a specific test suite—which the AI will work toward until met (ClaudeDevs, ClaudeDevs). This is supported by the "Ralph loop," which checks conditions against the transcript before allowing the AI to stop (ClaudeDevs), and "auto mode," which can be enabled via the desktop mode selector or CLI (ClaudeDevs). For recurring maintenance, Claude Code now supports `/schedule` for timed tasks like morning triage (ClaudeDevs) and `/loop` for iterative refactors and backlog tasks (ClaudeDevs). Additionally, a "stop hook" has been added to provide programmatic control over when the AI finishes, allowing integration with CI endpoints (ClaudeDevs). Simultaneously, OpenAI's Codex has enhanced its testing suite by integrating an in-app browser that can validate apps across various viewport sizes and breakpoints (OpenAIDevs). To streamline this process, Codex can disable animations to speed up testing by 1-2x and provides screenshots of key moments for user verification (OpenAIDevs). OpenAI has also optimized the tool by making annotations faster and reducing token consumption (OpenAIDevs). To improve developer workflow, Codex now allows users to open multiple full windows in parallel, use a `/side` command to fork conversations for follow-up questions, and set hotkeys for the pop-up window (OpenAIDevs). These updates reflect a broader shift toward "agentic" coding, where AI does more than suggest lines of code but actively manages the testing and execution phases. This trend is further evidenced by the availability of a research preview for "Fast mode" for Opus 4.7 across several platforms, including Cursor, v0, and Windsurf (ClaudeDevs).
