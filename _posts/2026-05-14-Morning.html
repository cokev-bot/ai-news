Summary: The artificial intelligence sector continues to drive massive financial valuations and urban transformation, even as societal and geopolitical tensions mount. Despite this growth, there are growing concerns regarding the long-term economic and regulatory impact of the technology. Furthermore, there is a pressing call for the creation of institutions to protect the public from both the state and tech companies, highlighting a desperate need for more "adult supervision" in AI development (FT AI). These systemic worries are echoed in the financial sector, where questions are being raised about whether an AI spending plateau is approaching (FT AI). The cultural and geopolitical landscape reflects a similarly volatile relationship with AI. In the U.S., the creative industry is seeing a rise in "microdramas"—low-budget, vertical, short-form content that has grown into a billion-dollar market and a potential lifeline for Hollywood creators (NYT AI). However, public sentiment remains mixed; at the University of Central Florida, humanities students booed a commencement speaker who characterized AI as the "next industrial revolution" (NYT AI, NYT AI). Meanwhile, the race for AI dominance is being shaped by divergent perspectives between superpowers, though analysts suggest the primary issues may be domestic rather than stemming from China (NYT AI).

AI Labs

Summary: Leading AI labs are aggressively expanding their agentic capabilities, shifting the focus from simple chat interfaces toward autonomous agents capable of executing complex workflows and interacting with software environments. Baidu is pivoting its strategy to embrace the "Agent Era," establishing "Daily Active Agents" as a primary key metric for success (BaiduAI_News). This push is exemplified by the launch of the DuMate mobile app, an AI-native agent designed for multi-step workflows that synchronizes tasks in real time between PCs and mobile devices (BaiduAI_News). Coding agents are seeing significant commercial and technical deployment. Baidu recently released the Miaoda App and Miaoda Enterprise Edition, noting that 90% of the Miaoda App's own code was generated by the agent itself (BaiduAI_News). These agents have already served over 10 million users, with the total value of generated applications reaching RMB 5 billion (BaiduAI_News). Meanwhile, OpenAI has addressed the security challenges of deploying coding agents on Windows by developing a specialized sandbox for Codex, aiming to balance developer utility with machine access safety (OpenAIDevs). Further enhancing agent autonomy, Kimi has introduced the "Kimi Web Bridge" browser extension, which allows agents to interact with websites by searching, scrolling, clicking, and typing (Kimi_Moonshot). This tool supports various interfaces including Claude Code, Cursor, and Codex (Kimi_Moonshot, Kimi_Moonshot). Powered by K2.6's multimodal capabilities, Kimi agents can now navigate and replicate websites, create Google Forms automatically through chat, and transform daily user workflows into reusable skills (Kimi_Moonshot, Kimi_Moonshot, Kimi_Moonshot). These developments signal a broader industry movement toward an "open agent economy," a topic recently discussed between the dAI team and MiniMax_AI in Shanghai (MiniMax_AI). From specialized plugins like the JumpFoundry Hermes Agent for glyph generation (Kimi_Moonshot) to cross-platform automation that fills spreadsheets (Kimi_Moonshot), the trajectory of AI is moving rapidly toward agents that function as autonomous operators across the digital ecosystem.

Developers

Summary: OpenAI is expanding the accessibility of its coding capabilities by bringing Codex to the Windows environment. To facilitate this integration, the company focused on solving the tension between providing coding agents with sufficient machine access and maintaining developer security (OpenAIDevs). To address these challenges, OpenAI developed a specialized Windows sandbox for Codex (OpenAIDevs). This architecture is designed to ensure that coding agents remain useful to the developer without requiring a constant stream of approval prompts or granting the agent unrestricted access to the entire machine (OpenAIDevs). This development represents a strategic step in balancing the autonomy of AI coding agents with the safety requirements of a local operating system (OpenAIDevs).
