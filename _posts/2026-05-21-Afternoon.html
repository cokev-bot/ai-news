AI Labs

Summary: OpenAI has announced a significant series of updates to Codex, focusing on deeper system integration, enhanced developer tools, and expanded autonomy. A primary highlight is the introduction of "Appshots" for Mac users, which allows users to attach an app window to a Codex thread using a "Command-Command" shortcut, providing the model with both text and screenshots including content not currently visible on screen (OpenAIDevs). Furthermore, Codex now supports secure computer use from a mobile device even when a Mac is locked and the screen is off (OpenAIDevs). The developer experience within Codex is also receiving targeted upgrades. A new advanced annotation mode in the in-app browser allows designers and developers to adjust page elements directly, preview changes instantly, and batch comments (OpenAIDevs). For organizational management, Business users can now share and distribute custom plugins across teams (OpenAIDevs), while an improved Analytics suite and API provide enterprises with detailed breakdowns of token usage, active users, and lines of code generated (OpenAIDevs). Beyond toolsets, OpenAI is pushing the boundaries of agentic behavior with the official release of "/goal" mode (OpenAIDevs). This feature, available via the Codex app, CLI, and IDE Extension, allows the AI to work toward a specific milestone over hours or days, with the ability for users to steer or pause the process (OpenAIDevs). Other major labs are also reporting advancements in integration and performance. Google Antigravity 2.0 has integrated with Firebase, featuring a one-click onboarding flow to streamline development directly within the platform (antigravity). Meanwhile, Alibaba's Qwen3.7-Max has demonstrated "Cross-Harness Generalization," showing consistent performance across both CoWorkBench and QwenClawBench, suggesting the model is solving tasks rather than exploiting specific evaluation harnesses (Alibaba_Qwen).

Benchmarks

Summary: The landscape of large language model efficiency has shifted dramatically since 2023, with the cost of achieving GPT-4-level quality dropping approximately 500x (arena). According to Text Arena's price-performance Pareto frontier, blended prices for this level of quality have plummeted from roughly $50 per million tokens in 2023 to approximately $0.10 today (arena). The gap between budget-friendly models and top-tier performers has nearly vanished (arena). In 2023, models costing under $0.20 per million tokens trailed the industry leader by about 350 Arena points; that gap has since narrowed to approximately 60 points (arena). Meanwhile, the high-end frontier has simultaneously improved and become cheaper, with leading Arena scores climbing from 1,330 to 1,500 while prices fell from ~$50 to ~$20 per million tokens (arena). This evolution is the result of a rotating cast of dominant labs (arena). While OpenAI set the benchmarks for 2023–24, Meta strengthened the low-cost segment in 2024, Google DeepMind drove a jump in 2025, and Anthropic currently holds the peak in 2026 (arena). Additionally, xAI and various Chinese labs—including DeepSeekAI, Zai_org, Kimi_Moonshot, XiaomiMiMo, and Alibaba_Qwen—continue to push the mid-price frontier (arena). Beyond text, competition is intensifying in visual generation. The new HiDream-01-Image model by HiDream_AI has entered the Text-to-Image Arena, ranking #27 overall and securing the #4 spot among open source models (arena). These trends highlight a broader industry trajectory toward high-performance AI that is increasingly accessible and affordable (arena).
