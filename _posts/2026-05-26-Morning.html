Summary: The global AI landscape is shifting from a pure software race toward a strategic struggle for physical infrastructure and specialized talent. Global mergers and acquisitions are currently dominated by a race to control compute, fiber networks, and energy sources (FT AI). This infrastructure push is meeting localized resistance, exemplified by concerns over a proposed data center complex spanning the equivalent of 3,800 football fields (NYT AI). Simultaneously, the war for talent is intensifying in China, where ByteDance is utilizing special stock tied to its AI business unit to prevent poaching (FT AI). Financial markets are recalibrating their approach to the AI boom, moving away from general software in favor of semiconductors (FT AI). However, this rapid expansion has drawn warnings from the European Central Bank, which suggests that the private-credit fueled AI boom could pose a risk to the financial system if the technology fails to meet its projected potential (FT AI). To manage this complexity, companies are increasingly turning to model exchanges; OpenRouter, which assists firms in selecting from hundreds of models for specific tasks, recently raised $113 million with backing from an Alphabet investment arm (NYT AI). As AI integrates into societal functions, critical frictions are emerging regarding ethics, labor, and governance. A Stanford-led study has identified "clear racial disparities" in AI-driven hiring, noting that candidates who fail these tests face "systemic rejection" across multiple companies (FT AI). In the creative sector, Spotify is pushing the boundaries of AI-generated music through a deal with Universal that allows for "controlled" covers and remixes (FT AI), sparking ongoing debates about whether AI stories are inherently "not art" (FT AI). On the regulatory and moral front, the Vatican has issued a cautionary signal. Pope Leo XIV released an encyclical, "Magnifica Humanitas," warning technology giants and urging AI leaders to slow down and consider the perils of the technology (NYT AI, NYT AI). While some observers describe the encyclical as "disappointingly mild" (NYT AI), it represents a high-level attempt to inject ethical restraint into a race that currently prioritizes speed and scale (NYT AI).
