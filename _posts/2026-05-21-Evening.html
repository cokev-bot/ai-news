Developer Tools

Summary: Google is significantly expanding its ecosystem of developer tools and accessible AI models, headlined by the global, free rollout of Gemini 3.5 Flash (GeminiApp). This push toward broader accessibility is paired with new agentic capabilities, such as the Gemini API's Managed Agents, which recently enabled RampLabs to build advanced finance agents without requiring backend infrastructure modifications (googleaidevs). Further enhancing the builder experience, Google has introduced updates to Stitch, an agentic AI design partner (googleaidevs). The tool now allows developers to move from an idea to a live mobile or web app UI in a single flow, featuring live-streamed design builds for real-time edits and the ability to import existing codebases or Design.md files to maintain brand consistency (googleaidevs). Additionally, Stitch can now generate dynamic, moving interfaces and export projects directly to live, shareable URLs (googleaidevs). Other updates within the Google AI suite include the emergence of Gemini Omni Flash, as seen in early cinematic macro-shot generation attempts (GoogleAI). Meanwhile, NotebookLM has returned to full service for research and learning after experiencing a period of system issues that caused temporary restrictions for some paid users (NotebookLM). Together, these developments signal a shift toward "agentic" workflows—where AI does more than generate text, but actively manages infrastructure, designs kinetic interfaces, and deploys live products (googleaidevs, googleaidevs).
