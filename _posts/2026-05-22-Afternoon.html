AI Labs

Summary: Major AI labs are prioritizing the advancement of agentic capabilities and coding performance with a series of new model releases. Google AI Studio has introduced Gemini 3.5 Flash, a model designed to provide frontier performance for coding and agents, specifically targeting complex, long-horizon tasks with real-world utility (GoogleAIStudio). Similarly, Alibaba's Qwen3.7-Max has shown strong performance across coding agent benchmarks, significant improvements in general-purpose agents, and exceptional strength in multilingualism and hard reasoning benchmarks (Alibaba_Qwen). The ecosystem for AI agents is further evolving through strategic integrations and tool refinements. MiniMax Agent has transitioned to using Perplexity Search after benchmarking three AI-native search providers across more than 700 agent tasks (MiniMax_AI). This shift to Perplexity resulted in a 45% reduction in tool calls per task, a 42% decrease in token usage, and a 2% increase in pass rates compared to their previous default, Serper (MiniMax_AI). Additionally, Antigravity has released version 2.0, which includes fixes for Windows IDE connectivity and clearer instructions for restoring IDE settings and extensions following user feedback (antigravity). Beyond technical updates, labs are adjusting their accessibility and incentive structures. DeepSeek has announced that the discount for DeepSeek-V4-Pro is now permanent, extending the offer until May 31, 2026 (deepseek_ai). Meanwhile, Antigravity has reset weekly Gemini quotas for all users as a gesture of goodwill following the rollout of their IDE updates (antigravity).
