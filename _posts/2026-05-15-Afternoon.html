Benchmarks

Summary: The infrastructure behind AI model evaluation is becoming increasingly sophisticated to handle the massive scale of user interactions. Researchers Guanglei Song and I-Hung Hsu from Arena have detailed the complex data pipeline that powers Arena's category leaderboards, which now process millions of votes per week (arena). To categorize evaluations across various domains—including text, image, and frontend coding—Arena utilizes a pipeline consisting of Databricks and hourly Spark jobs (arena). A central component of this system is a pluggable tagger framework that leverages LLMs to assign metadata to evaluations, a process that enhances the utility of the data for research purposes beyond simple leaderboard rankings (arena). The technical architecture is designed for scalability and resilience, featuring dynamic concurrency control to manage flaky LLM APIs and the ability to add new taggers without requiring a full system rebuild (arena). Furthermore, the team has implemented cost-control measures through model selection, idempotency, and filtering, while maintaining the ability to backfill historical data alongside live streams (arena). These insights into the data pipeline are available via detailed whiteboard sessions on YouTube (arena), and the resulting performance metrics can be explored directly on the Arena leaderboards (arena).
